Hidden all my life and seeked something that doesn't exist

Camus used the myth of Sisyphus, a figure from Greek mythology condemned to endlessly roll a boulder up a hill only to watch it roll back down, as a metaphor for the human condition. He argued that despite the apparent meaninglessness of Sisyphus' task, he can find a sense of purpose and fulfillment in embracing the absurdity of his situation and choosing to continue the task anyway.

In a way, Camus' philosophy of the absurd suggests that while life may lack ultimate meaning, individuals have the freedom to create their own meaning and purpose through their actions and choices. This notion challenges traditional philosophical and religious views about the meaning of life, but it has resonated with many people who find a sense of empowerment and liberation in accepting the absurdity of existence.
